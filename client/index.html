1. Diesel exhaust from the engine enters the system and passes through a diffuser plate to smooth out the flow. 2. The exhaust passes through a CRT catalyst coated with platinum. Oxidation occurs with Carbon monoxide becoming carbon dioxide, hydrocarbons turning to carbon dioxide and water and proportion of nitrogen dioxide is increased. 3. next, it passes through a CRT filter, trapping particulates. The particulates react with nitrogen dioxide, creating carbon dioxide and water. 4. Urea is injected into the exhaust in the mixing section. The urea rapidly decomposes to ammmonia. 5. Gases then pass through the SCR catalyst where the ammonia breaks down the nitrogen oxides to nitrogen and water. 6. A final cleanup catalyst turns any remaining ammonia to nitrogen and water vapour. {% endmarkdown %} {% endblock %}
